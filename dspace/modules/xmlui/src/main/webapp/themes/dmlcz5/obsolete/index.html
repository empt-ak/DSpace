Journals

  • Acta Universitatis Carolinae. Mathematica et Physica

    Acta Universitatis Carolinae, Mathematica et Physica (abbr. AUC) was a periodic journal which appeared twice a year, containing altogether approx. 180 pages. It was designated for publication of papers written by members and guests of the Faculty Mathematics and Physics of Charles University. Special regard was given to research papers from mathematics and physics, but attractive survey papers were also accepted. The journal ceased publication in 2013, the last published issue being 2013/1.

  • Acta Universitatis Palackianae Olomucensis. Facultas Rerum Naturalium. Mathematica

    The journal Acta Universitatis Palackianae Olomucensis, Facultas rerum naturalium, Mathematica, publishes original research papers from mathematical analysis, algebra, geometry, statistics and numerical methods in English.

  • Applications of Mathematics

    Applications of Mathematics publishes original research papers of high scientific level which are directed towards applications in different branches of science. Among the areas covered are, in particular, engineering problems, solution of differential and integral equations related to applications, mathematical physics, optimization, probability, mathematical statistics, etc. Scientific papers by the authors from the whole world are published in congress languages (mostly in English), sometimes a few pages are devoted to book reviews and news. Till 1990, the journal was published under the title Aplikace Matematiky.

  • Archivum Mathematicum

    Archivum Mathematicum is a quarterly periodical published by the Department of Mathematics, Faculty of Science of Masaryk University, Brno, Czech Republic. It contains important research papers in mathematics.

  • Časopis pro pěstování matematiky

    Časopis pro pěstování matematiky was a quarterly journal devoted to publications of peer reviewed mathematical papers of high scientific quality. It was a successor to the original journal "Časopis pro pěstování mathematiky a fysiky" (Journal for Cultivation of Mathematics and Physics) founded in 1872. Since 1991 it has been published under the title Mathematica Bohemica.

  • Časopis pro pěstování matematiky a fysiky

    Časopis pro pěstování matematiky a fysiky (Journal for Cultivation of Mathematics and Physics) published by the Union of Czech Mathematicians and Physicists was the very first mathematical journal in the lands of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. The journal presented original papers in mathematics and physics as well as information on recent research progress and on events in national and foreign scientific life. Starting in 1872 the journal continued till 1950 ecxept of the forced interruption during the occupation of Czechoslovakia in World War II. Since 1882 the journal contained a special supplement for high-school students. In 1951, the Czechoslovak Academy of Sciences took over the journal and split it into two journals, Časopis pro pěstování matematiky (Journal for Cultivation of Mathematics) and Československý časopis pro fyziku (Czechoslovak Journal for Physics).

  • Commentationes Mathematicae Universitatis Carolinae

    Commentationes Mathematicae Universitatis Carolinae (founded by E. Čech) is a quarterly periodical published by the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics of Charles University, Prague, Czech Republic. It contains important research articles and a limited number of invited survey articles, covering pure as well as applied mathematics.

  • Communications in Mathematics

    Despite a new name, Communications in Mathematics is not a new publication – it has a 17 year tradition, being a continuation of the journal Acta Mathematica et Informatica Universitatis Ostraviensis, founded in 1993, and later published under the title Acta Mathematica Universitatis Ostraviensis (2003–2009). Communications in Mathematics publishes original research papers and survey papers in pure and applied mathematics, preferebly in the following areas: Number theory, Algebraic structures, Combinatorics, Cryptography, Differential geometry, Global analysis, Differential equations, Control and Optimization, Calculus of variations, Mathematical physics, Fuzzy logic and fuzzy set theory. To be acceptable for publication papers must make significant, original and correct contributions to one or more of the topics within these areas. High quality review papers of interest to a wide range of scientists in mathematics and its applications are equally welcome. The journal is reviewed in Mathematical Reviews and Zentralblatt MATH.

  • Czechoslovak Mathematical Journal

    Czechoslovak Mathematical Journal publishes original research papers in mathematics. Although under a new title, the present journal forms a continuation of the renowned Časopis pro Pěstování Mathematiky a Fysiky which has been published by the Union of Czech Mathematicians and Physicists since 1872.

  • Kybernetika

    Kybernetika is a bi-monthly international journal dedicated to the rapid publication of high-quality, peer-reviewed research articles in the fields covered by its title. The journal is published by the Institute of Information Theory and Automation (UTIA). Kybernetika traditionally publishes research results in the fields of Control Systems, Information Sciences, System Sciences, Statistical Decision-Making, Applied Probability Theory, Random Processes, Fuzziness and Uncertainty Theories, Operations Research and Theoretical Computer Science, as well as in topics closely related to these fields.

  • Mathematica Bohemica

    Mathematica Bohemica publishes papers of high scientific quality which bring new mathematical results. It is a successor to the original journal Casopis pro pestovani mathematiky a fysiky (Journal for Cultivation of Mathematics and Physics) founded in 1872.

  • Mathematica Slovaca

    The aim of this journal is to publish original research papers in all areas of mathematics containing new substantial and significant results or methods, with complete proofs. Contributions presented to the journal can also be short notes, surveys and possibly research problems. Occasionally the journal publishes book reviews, notices and news about topical events interesting to the mathematical community. In addition to regular issues, supplementary issues are published focusing on a theme of current interest, honoring an individual, or containing proceedings of a conference.

  • Pokroky matematiky, fyziky a astronomie

    The journal presents original as well as translated survey and informatory papers devoted to history and present of mathematics, physics and astronomy and to instruction of these fields. The journal is intended to broad public in mathematics and physics. Besides scholarly papers it brings also book reviews and information about activities in the Union of Czech Mathematicians and Physicists.

Monographs

  • DML

    Mathematicians dream of a digital archive containing all peer-reviewed mathematical literature ever published, properly linked and validated/verified. The objectives of DML workshops were to formulate the strategy and goals of a global mathematical digital library and to summarize the current successes and failures of ongoing technologies and related projects.

  • EQUADIFF

    International Conference on Differential Equations and their Applications. Topics: Theory and applications of differential equations in the broad sense (ordinary differential equations, partial differential equations, numerical methods and applications). The conference organized by rotation in Praha (1962, 1977, 1989, 2001), Bratislava (1966, 1981, 1993, 2005) and Brno (1972, 1985, 1997, 2009) became the worlds oldest series of comprehensive conferences on differential equations.

  • NAFSA

    The series of Spring Schools has been organized in four years period since 1978 with the aim to bring together a broad audience including experienced mathematicians as well as students and to offer them extensive surveys of recent developments in the named fields delivered by distinguished specialists.

  • PANM

    The first seminar Programs and Algorithms of Numerical Mathematics took place in 1983, the second one in 1984. Since then it has been organized biannualy in different places in the Jizera Mountains in North Bohemia. The objective of this series of seminars is to provide a forum for presenting and discussing advanced topics in numerical analysis, computer implementation of algorithms, new approaches to mathematical modeling, and also multiprocessor applications of computational methods.

  • Toposym

    International Conference on General Topology and its Relations to Modern Analysis and Algebra. Held in Prague every 5 years since 1961.

  • WSAA

    The first Winter School in Abstract Analysis took place in 1973. It was founded by Zdeněk Frolík (1933–1989). This international School gained a popularity among the people interested in general topology, functional analysis, measure theory and combinatorics. Later the School was divided the into two sections, one from Analysis, the other from Topology. Some participants created later on an independent Winter School Geometry and Physics. The organizers ceased publishing WSAA proceedings in 2010.

  • WSGP

    Winter School „Geometry and Physics“ arose inside the Winter School of Abstract Analysis. Geometry and Physics firstly emerged as a subsection of the section of topology in 1981 and since 1985 it became an independent international conference. The name of the schools was adjusted in the early years and stabilized at Geometry Physics. Since 1994 the the conference is supported by International Erwin Schrödinger Institute for Mathematical Physics in Vienna.

Proceedings

  • Bolzano Collection

    Digitized book collection of the Czech mathematician and philosopher Bernard Placidus Johann Nepomuk Bolzano (1781-1848, Prague). From 1804 to 1817 Bolzano published several important mathematical works. In 1837 his "Wissenschaftslehre" appeared as an attempt to present a complete theory of science and knowledge. Approximately between 1830 and 1840 he worked on his major work "Grössenlehre" (remained unfinished). Around 1920 Martin Jašek discovered in Bolzano’s manuscripts an example of continuous nowhere differentiable functions. Bolzano’s work "Paradoxien des Unendlichen" was published three years after his death. His theories of mathematical infinity anticipated Cantor theory of sets. In addition to his mathematical work, Bolzano was also famous as a philosopher and as a logician.

  • Brana k vědění

    The series "Brána k vědění" (Gate to Knowledge) was founded by the Union of Czechoslovak Mathematicians and Physicists on the proposal of its director Miloslav Valouch (1878-1952). The editors were Vítězslav Jozífek (1905-1983) a František Kahuda (1911-1987), it was printed in the Union's printing works Prometheus. The series was meant for students and wide public interested in mathematics and physics. The DML-CZ presents only volumes with mathematical subjects.

  • Cesta k vědění

    The series "Cesta k vědění" (Path to Knowledge) was founded by the Union of Czech Mathematicians and Physicists on the impuls of its members in the year 1940. It was meant for high school students and graduates and other people interested in mathematics, physics and related fields. It played an important role for individual education particularly in the period 1940-1945, when the Czech universities were closed by the Nazis. The DML-CZ presents only volumes with mathematical subjects.

  • Dějiny matematiky

    The book series "History of Mathematics" has been established in 1994 to facilitate publication of both short and extensive texts from history of mathematics. The series already contains over 50 monographs, including proceedings of extended texts of lectures delivered at summer schools and seminars on history of mathematics held for secondary school teachers, as well as several PhD theses.

  • Kruh

    The series "Kruh" (Ring) was founded by the Union of Czechoslovak Mathematicians and Physicists in 1925. It published original works by Czech and Slovak mathematicians as well as translations of foreign works popularizing mathematics and physics. The DML-CZ presents only volumes with mathematical subjects.

  • Z dějin Jednoty českých matematiků a fyziků

    A number of publications has been related to the history of the Union of Czech Mathematicians and Physicists since its foundation in 1862 - jubilee almanacs and publications devoted to its development. Most of them are included in this collection "From the history of the Union of Czech Mathematicians and Physicists". Further publications can be found in the collection “Dějiny matematiky” and in the journal “Pokroky matematiky, fyziky a astronomie”.

Eminent Czech mathematicians

  • Borůvka, Otakar

    Otakar Borůvka (1899-1995) is one of the most prominent Czech mathematicians of the 20th century. He spent most of his professional life in Brno (working at Masaryk University and at the Mathematical Institute of the Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic). His extensive scientific work, i.e. 85 original scientific works and 5 monographs which have been translated into many languages, covers 5 mathematical areas: classical mathematical analysis, graph theory, differential geometry, algebra and theory of differential equations. Digital Archive of Otakar Borůvka presents the complete collection of Borůvka's works as well as the collection of works about Otakar Borůvka, his life and achievements.

  • Čech, Eduard

    Eduard Čech (1893-1960) was the greatest Czechoslovak mathematician of the 20th century. In his time he was one of the world's leading experts in topology and differential geometry and significantly contributed to the development of both fields. His scientific legacy includes more than one hundred original research papers and 14 monographs. In addition to these works, this digital archive presents also his numerous high school textbooks and other works for mathematics teaching, and also a collection of works about Eduard Čech himself, his life, work and achievements.

  • Cesta k vědění

    Vojtěch Jarník (1897-1970) influenced by both his scientific work and his teacher’s activities several generations of Czech and Slovak mathematicians. He was an expert in traditional fields of mathematical analysis and also one of the first Czech mathematicians who mastered set theory and topology, measure theory and the theory of integral. His work comprises about 90 scientific papers devoted to problems of lattice points, Diophantine approximations and geometry of numbers, the theory of real functions and some other topics, and 10 textbooks on calculus, theory of integral and mathematical analysis. Digital Archive of Vojtěch Jarník presents the collection of his scientific works, texbooks, reviews etc. as well as the collection of works about him.
